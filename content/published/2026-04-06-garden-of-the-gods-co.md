70 out of 100 on a 39°F April morning — that's honestly solid for Garden of the Gods in early spring. The big forecasted rain tomorrow is the thing to know about, but today's window is real.

Garden of the Gods in Colorado Springs is one of the most recognized landscapes in North America. The 300-million-year-old sandstone formations — some reaching 300 feet — stand vertical against a backdrop of Pikes Peak when conditions are right. The Main Loop is 3.5 miles with 300 feet of gain, rated easy, and is paved for most of its length. It's free. No reservation, no permit, no fee. That combination of accessibility, scale, and payoff makes this place relentlessly busy.

The conditions: 39°F right now, AQI 33 (clean Colorado Front Range air), winds at 7 mph. High of 70° today before that storm system moves in overnight — tomorrow's forecast shows 74% rain probability, so if you're in Colorado Springs and planning this, today is your day. The weather note in the park's guidelines says to watch for lightning and to exit the formations area immediately when storms develop. The red rock formations act as lightning conductors.

Riley's been here. Garden of the Gods is dog-friendly on leash throughout the park, and the paved main loop is easy on paws. He spent half the trip staring at the rock squirrels that treat the formations like their personal apartment complex. Keep dogs back from the rock faces — the wildlife doesn't need the stress and neither does your dog.

The crowds issue is real and the notes specifically say arrive before 9am. This is not a flexible suggestion. By 10am on weekends the North Parking Lot is full and the overflow lot fills shortly after. The formations don't shrink, but the experience of walking through them with 200 other people doing the same thing is genuinely different from an early-morning visit when the light is angled and it's quiet. Go before 9am and you'll understand why this place has been drawing visitors since the 1880s.

Garden of the Gods is a city park managed by Colorado Springs — no federal land complications, no timed entry permits (unlike nearby Hanging Lake), just show up. The park is open daily and year-round. Trail surface is paved on the main loop with some packed gravel on the connector trails toward the back of the park.

Pikes Peak is visible from the central viewpoint when the sky is clear. At 39°F with AQI 33, you've got that view today. Go get it.

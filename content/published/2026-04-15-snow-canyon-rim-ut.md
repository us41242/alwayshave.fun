## Why the Urgency

Snow Canyon's official season is **October through April**. We are in the last two usable weeks.

This trail runs along a slickrock rim in red-and-white Navajo Sandstone just outside St. George. In April it's a postcard — wildflowers in the wash, the rock still cool enough to scramble on, highs in the 70s. In June that same trailhead hits 105°F and the rock becomes a frying pan. You're not hiking Snow Canyon Rim in July. Nobody is.

This weekend and next are the window. After that, it's a pre-sunrise trail until October.

## The Trail Itself

3.8 miles out and back, 600 feet of gain. On paper it reads easy. In practice it plays harder because a chunk of it is slickrock scrambling — no dirt tread, just blazes painted on sandstone and your own sense of where the route wants to go.

The rim section is the payoff. You're walking a sandstone fin above the canyon floor with the whole park below you — the West Canyon dunes, the petrified dunes beyond, the lava fields off to the north. On a clear April day you can see Pine Valley Mountain still holding snow in the distance.

## Riley's Trail

Snow Canyon is one of the better dog-friendly state parks in the Southwest. Leash required, bring your own water, pick up after your dog — same drill as anywhere.

Two things on Riley specifically:

- **Rattlesnake season is back.** The trail notes flag it and I'll flag it again. The snakes are waking up in April and they like the warm slickrock as much as your dog does. Keep the leash tight through tall grass sections and watch your dog's nose.
- **Paws are fine right now.** In three weeks they won't be. Back-of-the-hand test on the rock — if you can't hold it there for five seconds, he's not walking on it.

## Logistics

Snow Canyon State Park has a **$15 per vehicle day-use fee**. Cash or card at the entrance booth. No timed entry, no permit lottery. You drive in, you pay, you park.

Three trailheads access the Rim Trail. The cleanest start is at the **Whiptail Trailhead** — it's the lower lot with the bigger parking area. Fills by 9 on weekends. Get there by 8 and you're fine.

There's no shade on the rim section. Carry more water than you think — 2 liters each in April, 3 in May. No water on trail.

## Jake's Take

Snow Canyon is one of those trails that Utah locals know about and out-of-staters drive past on their way to Zion. It's 45 minutes from St. George, the drive in is paved all the way, and you're looking at the same Navajo Sandstone that makes Zion famous — just without the shuttle, without the permit, and without the tour buses.

If you've got a Zion trip booked this spring and you want a warm-up day, or a backup day when Angels Landing permits sell out, or just a dog-friendly sandstone hike that doesn't require you to schedule your life around Recreation.gov — this is it.

Go this weekend. Go next weekend. By the first week of May you'll be starting at 5 AM and wrapping by 9 to beat the heat, and that's a different hike.

If you're on the fence — go.

## The Quickest Drive from Vegas to a 100/100 Trail

I've said it before and I'll keep saying it: people fly into Las Vegas, spend three days inside a casino breathing recycled air, and never realize there's a world-class hiking area 45 minutes from the Strip. Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area exists. Calico Hills exists. And today it's scoring a perfect 100.

Conditions: 64°F, winds calm, AQI Good, zero rain. This is what spring in the Mojave looks like when it's behaving itself.

## Conditions Breakdown

**Temperature:** 64°F and climbing slowly. Red Rock sits at around 3,600 feet — noticeably cooler than the Las Vegas valley floor, especially in the morning. You want a light layer at the trailhead. By 10am you're shedding it.

**Wind:** Light. The Calico Hills area is slightly sheltered compared to the higher Red Rock peaks, but wind can pick up quickly in the afternoon. Today it's calm. Go in the morning if you can.

**Rain:** Zero chance. Desert rain brings its own reward — the post-rain sandstone color at Red Rock is genuinely insane, deep red and saturated — but dry conditions mean stable footing on the slickrock scrambles.

**AQI:** Good. Dust is the main air quality concern in the Mojave, especially in spring when winds pick up. Not an issue today.

## What to Expect on the Trail

The Calico Hills loop is around 3.5 miles with minimal elevation gain — it's genuinely accessible for a wide range of fitness levels. The star of the show is the scrambling. The Calico Hills are made of Aztec sandstone, the same bright red and white banded rock you see throughout Red Rock, and large sections of the trail take you up and over the rock itself.

This isn't a trail where you stare at a path. It's a trail where you navigate. There are cairns, some worn paths, and plenty of places to just climb wherever looks interesting. Take your time. Bring your camera. Stop and look back toward Las Vegas — the contrast between city and desert never gets old.

## Dog-Friendly?

Yes, and this is one of the genuinely great dog hikes in Nevada. Riley loses his mind here. BLM-managed land under a conservation area designation means leashed dogs are welcome on all trails. The sandstone scrambles are easy enough for most dogs but watch their paws in summer heat — the rock can get very hot. April is perfect dog weather.

Bring extra water for them. There's no water on trail.

## Jake's Take

Calico Hills is my default recommendation for anyone in Vegas who asks "is there actually anything to do outside here?" It's close enough to not feel like a commitment, the trail is interesting enough to not feel like a checkbox, and the views — both of the red rock formations and back toward the city — are legitimately impressive.

Park at the Calico Hills I or II trailhead on the Red Rock Scenic Loop Drive. The loop drive itself is one-way and 13 miles, so plan your parking based on where you want to start. Red Rock entrance fee is $15/vehicle (or America the Beautiful pass works here).

Arrive before 9am if you can — the loop drive and trailheads fill up fast on weekends, and NPS has started using a reservation system for the Scenic Loop on busy days. Check the Red Rock Canyon NCA website before you go.

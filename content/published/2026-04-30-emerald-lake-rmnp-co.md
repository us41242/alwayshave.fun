Emerald Lake is **40/100** today and that's not a soft number. This is a "wait it out" day, not a "tough it out" day. The trail is open, but every line on the dashboard is telling you the same thing: don't.

Here's what we're looking at: **26°F**, feels like **14°F**, wind **12 mph** with **20 mph gusts**, AQI **105 (Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups)**, and a **97% rain chance** that at 10,000 feet of elevation in late April is straight-up a snowstorm. The forecast for Friday (5/1) holds 85% precip and a high of 33°F. The first day this clears up is **Saturday (5/2)** with a 20% chance and a 42°F high — predicted score back up to 68.

If you've got a flexible weekend, you already know what to do. Skip today. Skip tomorrow. Aim for Saturday or Sunday.

If you don't have flexibility, the rest of this is what you need to know to either bail safely or — if you're going anyway — not get killed.

## NO DOGS — and Why That's Non-Negotiable

Before we get into the weather, the first thing: **Rocky Mountain National Park does not allow dogs on trails.** Not a "use caution" rule. Not a "leashed only" rule. A flat ban on every backcountry trail in the park, including Emerald Lake. Park rangers will write you a citation and send you back to the parking lot.

Mark and Hank love a good Colorado morning. They're not coming to RMNP today, and they wouldn't be coming if it were 75 and sunny. Kelly and I have hit Emerald Lake plenty of times over the years and the boys have always stayed home for that one. If you've got dogs and you want a Front Range hike on a day that's actually walkable, the Boulder OSMP trails are mostly leashed-and-okay, and Mt Falcon and the Roxborough State Park system allow dogs. Save those for Saturday. Today, even those will be ugly.

## What 40/100 Actually Means at Emerald Lake

The score is doing three jobs at once: weather, air, and trail conditions. Let me break out which is pulling it down.

**Air quality is the biggest factor today.** AQI 105 is well into the orange zone — Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups. PM2.5 is the pollutant of concern, which means there's particulate in the air. At RMNP this time of year that's almost always either an early-season prescribed burn or smoke drifting in from somewhere up the chain. Either way, working hard for 3.6 miles and 655 feet of gain in 105 AQI air is the kind of thing your lungs remember tomorrow. The dashboard flagged "N95 mask recommended" for a reason. If you have asthma or any respiratory condition, this is a hard no for today.

**Weather is the second factor.** A 97% precip chance at this elevation in this temperature range means snow, and probably a meaningful amount of it. The current temps are barely manageable for a trained-up winter hiker. The bigger problem is what happens during the hike — you start at 9 AM in 26°F and rain mixed with snow, you're 90 minutes in when the front intensifies and visibility drops below 100 feet, and now you're navigating the Bear Lake → Nymph → Dream → Emerald chain in a whiteout. The trail is well-trafficked and well-marked, but a whiteout in winter conditions on a 655-foot ascent is no joke even on a "moderate" trail.

**Wind is the third factor.** 12 mph sustained with 20 mph gusts is fine in summer. In a 26°F snowstorm above 10,000 feet, those gusts at the lake itself can hit you in the face hard enough to knock you off-balance on icy rocks. People underestimate wind in shoulder-season RMNP. Don't.

## If You're Going Anyway

I am not going to talk you out of going if you're already committed. I'm going to make sure you don't end up needing a SAR callout. Here's the gear floor for Emerald Lake on a 40/100 day:

- **Microspikes minimum, full crampons better.** The granite slabs above Bear Lake are notoriously slick once they're snow-and-ice covered. The trail above Dream Lake is the worst section.
- **Waterproof hardshell, top and bottom.** A 97% rain/snow forecast doesn't care about your "water-resistant" insulating jacket.
- **N95 or KN95 for the AQI.** Carry it in your pocket and wear it on the climb. You can pull it down at the lake.
- **Headlamp.** Sunset is 7:56 PM but the cloud cover will make 4 PM feel like 6 PM. If anything goes sideways, you do not want to be navigating without light.
- **Real navigation.** Carry a paper map and a compass, or a GPS device with the trail downloaded offline. Phone GPS in a snowstorm, with cold-drained battery, on top of a granite slab — bad combo.
- **Ten Essentials.** This is the day they exist for. Fire-starting kit, emergency bivy, extra calories, water that won't freeze in your pack.
- **Tell someone.** Give a partner or a family member your start time, expected return time, and a check-in cutoff. RMNP cell coverage is spotty.

I want to go back to the score chart for a second because Saturday is genuinely a different trail. The forecast as of this morning has Saturday (5/2) at:

- High **42°F**, low 16°F
- Rain chance **20%**
- UV index **9** — bring sun protection

That's a real go-day. The granite slabs will still have ice in the shadowed sections, but you'll have visibility, manageable air, and a temperature window that lets you take photos at the lake without your fingers seizing up. The Bear Lake parking lot is a permit-required, timed-entry deal in the official Jun–Oct window, but in the late-April shoulder you should be able to drive in directly. Confirm at the RMNP conditions page before you commit — Bear Lake Road has been known to close for plowing on storm days.

If you're driving up from Denver or the Front Range, plan a 6 AM departure. The Bear Lake lot fills by 8 AM on weekend storm-clearing days because every weather-watcher in Colorado has the same thought you do.

## What to Do Today Instead

If you've got the boots laced and the day blocked off, don't waste it. Lower-elevation Front Range trails will be in better shape than RMNP today. The Boulder reservoir loop, the Marshall Mesa trails south of Boulder, and Roxborough State Park (where dogs ARE allowed, by the way, on leash) are all going to be in the 50–65 range today — wet, cold, but walkable. The thing you're avoiding is altitude and exposed wind, both of which Emerald Lake is going to hit you with.

Save the RMNP day for Saturday or Sunday. The lake will still be there, the chain of four lakes will still be one of the prettiest 3.6 miles in Colorado, and your photos will actually come out. Today is not the day, and there is nothing wrong with admitting that. The mountain doesn't care, and neither does Mark — he's already curled up on the couch, which is exactly where he should be.

Bottom line: **40/100 today, do not go.** Aim for **Saturday at 68/100** with the right gear and a 20% precip window. That's the move.

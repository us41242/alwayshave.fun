100 out of 100. 69°F. AQI 28 (crystal clean). Dog-friendly. Paved. No permit. Free.

The Hoover Dam Trail is the easy Vegas day hike that most people overlook because they're focused on Red Rock Canyon or Lake Mead, and they're missing a genuinely solid option. This is 3.4 miles with 320 feet of gain on a paved path along the Nevada side of Lake Mead connecting the Hoover Dam visitor area to the Alan Bible Visitor Center. The lake views are real. The dam views are real. And the whole thing takes about 90 minutes.

The forecast for the next three days: 93°/63° today (1% rain), 95°/64° tomorrow (0%), 94°/67° the day after (0%). That heat is high for April — high 90s is unusual this early — which makes today a morning-only situation. Get there by 9am, done by 11am, and you're not fighting the midday desert heat. April typically runs 75-80°F around Boulder City and Henderson, so today and this week are running warm. Still perfectly hikeable in the morning.

Riley comes on this one. The Hoover Dam Trail is dog-friendly — paved, well-maintained, and there's shade near the dam structure itself. He loves the water views and the wind coming off the lake. Leash required.

The trail starts from the Hacienda Hotel parking area or from the Nevada Welcome Center just past the dam on the Nevada side. No fee for the trail itself — just the dam parking if you use that lot. The path parallels the lake for most of its length before reaching the visitor center.

What you're actually there to see: the scale of Hoover Dam is something you can't fully appreciate from a car window or the tourist observation decks. Walking the trail puts you alongside the Nevada wall of the dam at angles that show you how massive the structure actually is — 726 feet tall, 660 feet thick at the base. Built in 5 years in the 1930s. That context hits differently on foot.

There's no shade on most of the trail. The forecast high of 93°F means bring water even for a 90-minute hike. In the desert at 93°, you're sweating through whatever you brought before the turnaround. A liter each, minimum.

This is also one of the better easy hikes to bring visiting family. It's paved, it's accessible, the endpoint is the Hoover Dam visitor experience (the dam tour is worth doing), and the lake views along the way are legitimately impressive without requiring any hiking fitness. The 320-foot gain is gentle and spread over 3.4 miles.

No reservations, no fees, no permits. Just drive out from Vegas or Henderson (40 minutes) and go.

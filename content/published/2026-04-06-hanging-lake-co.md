Hanging Lake is sitting at a solid 70 out of 100 right now. That's good, not perfect, but it means you've got a narrow window to hit this iconic Colorado trail before things get complicated.

Right now it’s 39°F with basically no wind, 2 mph to be exact. That’s chilly, especially if you're hitting the trail early. Think layers. You'll warm up fast once you start climbing, but that first mile will be brisk. AQI is 38, which is crystal clear, and there’s zero chance of rain today. The air is fresh, and the views of Glenwood Canyon will be sharp. Fire risk is low, so no worries there.

So why isn't this a 100/100 day? The forecast. Today (Saturday) and Sunday morning look solid, but by Sunday afternoon, there's a 29% chance of rain. That's enough to make those steep, rocky sections slick, and honestly, standing at the lake in the rain isn't the vibe you want for one of the most unique hikes in Colorado. My advice: hit it Saturday or Sunday morning at the latest. Get up early, get it done. If you push it to Sunday afternoon, throw a lightweight rain jacket in your pack, just in case.

Let's talk about the hike itself. This is listed as "Hard" for a reason. It's only 2.8 miles, but you're gaining over a thousand feet of elevation in that short distance. It’s basically straight up the side of Glenwood Canyon. Don't let the short mileage fool you; you'll be feeling it in your quads. It’s not a death march, but it’s not a stroll through the park either. You’ll be navigating some rocky sections, narrow ledges, and a few boardwalks towards the top. But the payoff, that surreal turquoise water of Hanging Lake, is absolutely worth the effort.

Heads up on logistics. Hanging Lake is right off I-70 in Glenwood Canyon, just east of Glenwood Springs. The trailhead parking lot can fill up fast, even without the permit system in full effect. Get there early. Like, before 8 AM early. And speaking of permits: as of May 1st, you're going to need a timed entry permit to access the Hanging Lake trail, which you book through Recreation.gov. If you go this weekend (early April), you're actually ahead of the curve and don't need one yet. It’s a small window to do it without the extra step of booking ahead, so take advantage.

Now, about Riley... this is one of the rare ones he can't come on. Hanging Lake is strictly no dogs, which is a total bummer, I know. The rules are pretty strict because the ecosystem around the lake is super fragile. My buddy Kyle usually takes Riley to a dog park or one of the more dog-friendly trails near Glenwood Springs when we do this one, so keep that in mind if you're bringing your pup to the area.

That fragile ecosystem thing is real. The entire point of this hike is the lake, and it's extremely delicate. You'll see signs, but it's worth repeating: don't touch the water, don't step off the boardwalks, and definitely don't try to climb on the travertine formations. It’s a privilege to see it, and we all need to do our part to keep it pristine for the next person. Leave no trace, as always.

Seriously, this is one of those bucket-list Colorado hikes. You've got clear skies, no permit hassle yet, and conditions are good for a challenging climb. Don't let a little morning chill or a future rain forecast stop you from experiencing the beauty of Hanging Lake. Grab your crew, dress in layers, and get after it. You'll thank me when you're looking at that crazy turquoise water, knowing you beat the crowds and the rain. This is a solid chance to tick off a truly unique hiking experience. Go.

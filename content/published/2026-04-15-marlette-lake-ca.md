There is a quiet version of Lake Tahoe that only exists for about three weeks in April. The ski resorts have stopped spinning their lifts. The summer traffic from the Bay hasn't started yet. Spooner Summit, where Marlette Lake Trail begins, is still dusted with the last of the winter's snow in the shaded switchbacks — but the main tread is walkable if you know what you're doing. That's the window we're in right now.

Marlette Lake is scoring 70 out of 100 today, climbing to 88 tomorrow and 100 by Saturday. If you want the big, glassy, still-cold-enough-to-keep-the-crowds-away version of this trail, the next five days are the trip.

## Today's Conditions

**Temperature:** 32°F at the trailhead this morning, warming to a 41°F high tomorrow. Saturday and Sunday push into the high 40s and low 50s. The ridge is consistently 5–8 degrees colder than the readings below — pack accordingly.

**Wind:** 3 mph with gusts to 4. Still. The lake surface will be mirror glass at dawn.

**Rain:** 3% today, 8% tomorrow. A 38% chance Thursday brings a possible snow shower at elevation, but it clears for a picture-perfect 47°F Friday and 54°F Saturday with zero percent precip.

**AQI:** 24. Exceptionally clean — the winter snowpack scrubs the air in the basin and we're still early enough in the season that nothing's burning, no one's barbecuing at volume, and the lake acts as a humidifier for the whole basin.

**Fire risk:** Low. Nothing burning within 250 km.

**Gear flag:** insulation layer required. Trust it. A puffy that lives in your pack is the difference between a miserable lunch at the lake and a lingering one.

## The Shoulder-Season Reality

Marlette Lake is officially a June-through-October trail, and I want to be honest about why. The Flume Trail — the trail you'll be on for most of the route — sits around 7,500 to 8,000 feet on the east side of Lake Tahoe. It holds snow in shaded pockets well into May most years.

Right now you will encounter:

- **Dry, clean tread** on the south-facing switchbacks climbing from Spooner Summit
- **Snow patches** in the shaded stretches between the ridge and the lake basin — typically ankle-deep, post-holing possible in the late afternoon as it softens
- **A fully unfrozen Marlette Lake** with the north-end dam area still showing ice along the shore

Bring **microspikes** and stash them in your pack. You may not need them — I didn't on my last April trip — but the one time you hit a 30-foot stretch of glazed north-facing trail at 8 AM, you'll want them.

## Getting There — The AWD Note

The trail starts from **Spooner Summit** on the Nevada side of Highway 50. Spooner Lake State Park is your parking option — $10 day-use fee, check the NV State Parks site for current rates. There are **no cars beyond Spooner Summit** on the trail itself (this is the Flume Trail system — bike-legal, vehicle-closed).

The drive up from South Lake Tahoe or Carson City is clear and paved. The AWD flag in the trail data is really about spring mornings when the parking lot entrance can hold a thin ice layer before 9 AM. If you have AWD or 4WD, it's a non-issue. If you're in a 2WD rental, arrive after 10.

## Kipper Logistics

Dog-friendly on-leash — this is one of my favorite dog hikes in the basin, honestly. Kipper loves it because the trail surface is soft (crushed granite and dirt, not slickrock) and the lake itself has gentle, accessible shoreline at the far end for a proper mid-hike swim. Or post-thaw paddle in her case — April water at Marlette is around 38°F and she's more of a shoreline wader than a swimmer in spring.

A few specifics:

- **Water** — there's one reliable creek crossing around mile 3.5 that works for filtering in a pinch. I still pack her water. The lake itself is drinkable with a filter but I don't love the logistics of filling on a windy shore.
- **Mileage** — 10.5 miles out and back with 1,600 feet of gain is a big day for a smaller dog. Kipper does it, but I cut it short if she's flagging. The Flume Trail junction at mile 2.8 is a perfectly fine turnaround if you want a 5.6-mile day with the best views.
- **Bikes** — this is a bike-legal trail. Mountain bikes are frequent even in April. Keep the leash short on blind corners.

## Timing the Photograph

The Marlette Lake photograph — the one with the lake reflecting the Carson Range and Lake Tahoe peeking over the western ridge — is a **golden hour shot**. Not sunrise. Not midday.

Here's why: the trail climbs from Spooner Summit northwest to the lake. The lake sits in a bowl with Marlette Peak to the east and Genoa Peak behind you. At sunrise, the east ridge blocks the direct light on the lake itself. At golden hour (roughly 6:45–7:30 PM this time of year), the western sun hits the peaks behind the lake and lights them against the still water.

Plan a start around 2 PM from Spooner Summit. You'll hit the lake around 4:30–5 PM. Shoot until 7:30. Hike out in fading light — **bring a headlamp**, which is absolutely non-negotiable this time of year when sunset is 7:36 PM and you've got descent in cold air.

## Olivia's Tip

Stop at Spooner Lake on the way in. It's a five-minute detour from the trailhead, it's usually ice-free by mid-April, and the morning light on the aspens around the lakeshore is the kind of thing that sells a trip to friends who are wavering about whether to come up with you next weekend.

This is the trail where Tahoe is still Tahoe — before the shuttles, before the parking lot attendants, before the summer traffic on 50 turns a 45-minute drive from Carson City into two hours. Go while the basin is quiet. The lake will still be there in July, but so will three thousand other people.

There is a specific feeling you get standing at the base of Vernal Fall in mid-April, when the Merced is carrying the last month of Sierra snowmelt through a 317-foot notch of granite and the spray hits you sideways. It is not the same waterfall you'll visit in August. In April, Vernal Fall is *loud*. The mist soaks through fleece. People laugh on the staircase because there's no other reasonable response. That's what you're walking into this week.

The trade-off, which I want to be honest about: the Mist Trail is scoring a 60 out of 100 right now, and that "Use Caution" tag is earned. The staircase — those 600 granite steps from the footbridge to the top of the fall — is **genuinely slippery** when it's this wet. You need the right shoes and the right mindset.

## The Conditions Today

**Temperature:** 46°F at the trailhead, forecast high of about 52°F. You'll be cold in the shaded switchbacks and warmer on the open ledge sections. Bring a shell that actually repels water, not just a fleece.

**Rain:** 41% chance today, lower tomorrow. The Mist Trail is the one place in Yosemite where rain almost doesn't change the experience — you're already getting soaked from the fall.

**Wind:** 5 mph. Calm. The canyon is sheltered.

**AQI:** 36. Clear and beautiful. Visibility is excellent for photos, though your lens will fog from the mist if you stand anywhere near the top of the staircase.

**Fire risk:** Low. Nothing burning within 300+ km.

## The April Version of This Hike

The Mist Trail is 3.6 miles round trip to the top of Vernal Fall, with 1,000 feet of gain. On paper that's moderate. In April it plays harder than it reads, and here's why:

**The staircase is always wet.** From about the footbridge at mile 0.8 to the top of Vernal Fall, you're inside the spray cone. The granite steps are carved into the cliff and they have a handrail, but the steps themselves get polished slick. In April that polish is covered with a film of water and sometimes ice in the early morning shade.

**Wear trail runners or approach shoes with aggressive lugs.** Leave the Hokas at home. Leave anything with a smooth sole at home. This is the one trail where footwear matters more than fitness.

**Consider a light rain pant.** Your legs will get drenched. On a 46°F day, drenched legs mean cold legs.

**Poles are debatable.** I don't bring mine on this one — the handrail does more for you than a pole ever could, and the steps are too close together for a natural pole cadence.

## Timing

Start early. The valley floor sits in shadow until about 9am in April, and the staircase doesn't see direct sun until mid-morning. First light means dry-ish rock for the switchbacks up to the footbridge, but the staircase itself won't be meaningfully drier no matter when you go. The benefit of an early start is fewer people on the narrow sections.

The shuttle from Yosemite Village runs year-round to the Happy Isles trailhead. First bus is usually around 7am — double-check the day-of schedule at the visitor center or on the NPS site, since spring maintenance can shift things.

If you're only going to the footbridge, plan on 90 minutes round trip. To the top of Vernal Fall, budget 3 hours. To Nevada Fall (another 1.5 miles and 900 feet of gain from the Vernal overlook), 5 to 6 hours total and a full pack of snacks.

## Kipper Logistics

No dogs on this trail. No dogs on any trail above the valley floor in Yosemite, really. Kipper stayed home with my neighbor this week — I have a standing trade where I watch her beagle one weekend a month and she takes Kipper when I head into the park. If you don't have a dog-sitter set up for Yosemite trips, start asking now. Valley kennels fill up and the few boarding options in Mariposa book months out for spring weekends.

## What To Bring

- Trail runners or approach shoes with real lugs
- Shell jacket, pit-zips open
- Light rain pants or quick-dry pants
- **Dry bag for your phone and camera.** This is non-negotiable. I learned this the hard way in 2021.
- Microfiber towel for lenses
- Water (1 liter to Vernal, 2+ if you're continuing to Nevada Fall)

## Olivia's Tip

The best photo is not from the top of Vernal Fall. The best photo is from the footbridge at sunrise, when the fall is backlit and the mist turns into a vertical rainbow. It's maybe 15 minutes of light. Be at the bridge by 6:45am and you'll have the frame to yourself.

And here's the thing about a 60/100 day on the Mist Trail in April: it's not a perfect-conditions score because it's genuinely slick and cold. But the fall itself will never be louder, or fuller, or more alive than it is right now. Go carefully, go early, wear the right shoes, and the caution score becomes an asterisk on one of the best waterfall hikes in the country.

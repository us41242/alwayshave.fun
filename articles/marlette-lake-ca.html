Marlette Lake Trail: 88/100, Hit It This Weekend

by Jake April 6, 2026 Score: 88/100 at time of writing 🐾 Dogs welcome

Alright, listen up because the Marlette Lake Trail is sitting at an 88 out of 100 right now. That's a "Great day to go" in our book, and honestly, you should be planning your weekend around it.

Right now, it’s 42°F with barely any wind at 4 mph, and the air quality is 27 (which is basically like breathing pure, fresh mountain air). Zero chance of rain today, and that's just the start. Tomorrow, Sunday, and even Monday are looking even better. We’re talking mid-50s for highs, lows comfortably in the high 30s, and the same crystal-clear skies. The wind stays calm, just a whisper. This whole run is pretty much perfect for a solid day out. Tuesday has a slight 17% chance of rain, so your prime window for a dry, cool adventure is firmly set for this weekend and early next week. You don't get conditions like this often, especially this time of year.

Now, Marlette Lake. We call it "moderate," and it definitely earns that rating. You’re looking at 10.5 miles round trip with 1600 feet of gain. That's a serious day out, not a casual stroll around the neighborhood. The initial climb from Spooner Summit up to the lake is a consistent grind, a good test for the legs, whether you’re hiking or pedaling. But man, the payoff once you crest that ridge and see Marlette Lake in its own basin, calm and clear, is genuinely fantastic. And the descent back down, especially if you're on two wheels, is just pure fun. You fly past the views you earned on the way up, looking out over Spooner Lake and the whole Tahoe Basin.

The good news? This trail is bike-legal, and honestly, that’s how Riley and I usually tackle it. It completely changes the dynamic, turning a long hike into an exhilarating ride. Just remember, it’s April. The official "snow free" season for this trail usually kicks in around June, so while the forecast looks clear and warm for the weekend, keep an eye out for any lingering patches of ice or muddy sections, especially in shaded areas or on north-facing slopes early in the morning. Better to have solid boots or good knobby tires than a surprise slip. A little preparation goes a long way here.

Riley, my Australian Shepherd, absolutely loves this one. He's done it a bunch of times and it’s always a winner for him. There are plenty of spots for him to sniff around, and when you get to Marlette Lake itself, it’s a perfect spot for a quick drink and a dip (for him, not you, unless you really like freezing water). Just keep him leashed, you know the drill, especially with bikes sharing the trail. And pack extra water for both of you; it’s a long haul, and you both hydrate more than you think on a climb like that.

Logistics are straightforward: you park at Spooner Summit. Head up the service road from there – no cars are allowed beyond this point, so don't even try. We usually aim to get there early, around 8 AM, to beat any crowds and snag a good parking spot before it fills up. Plus, hitting the climb when it's still cool is always better. It's a full half-day commitment, easily four to five hours depending on your pace and how long you chill by the lake. This isn't one of those "quick out and back before brunch" trails. This is a "pack a big sandwich, a thermos of coffee, and some trail snacks" kind of trip. You'll earn that post-hike burger.

Seriously, an 88 out of 100 for Marlette Lake doesn't happen every week, especially this early in the season with such clear, calm weather. If you've been eyeing Lake Tahoe for a solid hike or a challenging ride that rewards you with incredible scenery without being overly technical, this is your shot. The conditions are prime, the air is crisp, and it’s calling your name. Grab your pack, or your bike, and get after it. You'll thank me later.

Calico Hills Is About to Get Too Hot — Go This Weekend

by Jake April 14, 2026 Score: 78/100 at time of writing 🐾 Dogs welcome

The Conditions Right Now

Calico Hills is at 78 out of 100 today, but that's the worst it's going to be all week.

Tomorrow jumps to 90. Thursday, Friday, Saturday — all 90/100. 65°F highs, zero rain, UV at 8. If you've been telling yourself you'll get out to Red Rock "sometime this spring," this is the week. I'm not hedging on this one.

Why the Urgency

Read the fine print on the trail: best months Oct through April. You've got maybe two usable weekends left before the desert flips and everything west of the 215 becomes a convection oven. By May the surface temp on that sandstone is genuinely dangerous for dogs, and by June nobody's hiking Calico Hills for fun — they're hiking it for the "I survived" photo.

This weekend is the last clean shot at the version of Calico Hills you actually want: cool enough to climb hard, warm enough to scramble in shorts, light enough at 7:30pm to catch the red rock go orange before you hit the car.

Conditions Breakdown

Temperature: 65°F tomorrow, creeping to 68°F by Friday. Nights still dropping to the low 40s. Perfect layering weather — start in a shell, ditch it at the first scramble.

Wind: 16 mph steady, gusts 18. Nothing you'll notice once you're off the ridge.

Rain: 2% tomorrow, 0% Friday. The sandstone has had time to dry from earlier in the week. Friction is good.

AQI: 54, Moderate. Not perfect — ozone is starting to bump up as the Vegas valley warms — but well below anything that should keep you home unless you've got asthma.

Fire risk: Low. Nearest fire is 243 km away.

Riley's Trail

This is one of Riley's favorites and I'm not exaggerating. Calico Hills is one of the better dog-friendly trails within 30 minutes of the Strip, and the 6.8-mile loop has enough variety — slickrock scrambles, wash sections, shaded pockets — that he doesn't get bored. Bring double the water you think he needs. Desert math is different from your local greenbelt.

Couple notes if you're a dog owner:

  • Paws on warm sandstone are fine right now. In two weeks, they won't be. Test the rock with the back of your hand — five-second rule. If you can't hold your hand there, he can't walk on it.
  • No shade for long stretches. Start early.
  • The scenic drive loop has pullouts with water access at the visitor center, not on trail.

The Scenic Drive Permit Situation

Red Rock's scenic drive requires a timed-entry reservation October through May. Same system as the last few seasons — book it on recreation.gov. If you're just hitting Calico Hills via the Calico 1 or Calico 2 pullouts, you're on the scenic drive and you need the reservation. The visitor center lot is outside the fee area but that adds mileage.

Reservations for this weekend are releasing now in the 24–48 hour rolling window. Grab one tonight.

Jake's Take

Look — Red Rock is the Vegas hike that out-of-towners skip because they don't realize how close it is. 20 minutes from the Strip to the trailhead. You can finish the loop, shower, and be at dinner by 7. There's no other major metro where a world-class desert scramble is that close to a steakhouse.

I've hiked Calico Hills probably 40 times. In April. Between October and April. It never gets old, but it does get hot, and when it gets hot the magic is gone until October. The windows on either end of the season are the good stuff.

If you're on the fence — go. And if you've been meaning to bring out-of-town friends, this is the weekend. Next month you'll be apologizing for the heat.

Kanab Creek: 88/100, Hit This Slot Canyon

by Jake April 6, 2026 Score: 88/100 at time of writing 🐾 Dogs welcome

Okay, 88 out of 100. That's a "Great Day To Go" if you're keeping score at home, and for the Kanab Creek Wilderness in Northern Arizona, that's pretty damn good. This is your window for a serious slot canyon adventure in the Kaibab National Forest.

Let's break down that score. We're looking at 49 degrees Fahrenheit to start the day, with a barely-there 5 mph breeze. The air quality is a crystal-clear 36 AQI, and there's a big fat zero percent chance of rain. What does that mean on the ground? It means you start cool, you stay cool as you put in the miles, and by the time the sun's really cooking, you'll be deep in the canyon where it’s always a few degrees cooler anyway. You're looking at bluebird days all weekend for a 14-mile monster. The forecast for today shows highs around 76°F, which is perfect for working up a sweat without overheating, and the next two days follow suit with similar temps and practically no rain chance. This isn't a 100/100 like a rare winter desert day, but it’s an 88 because you get ideal morning temps and zero worries about the weather for a serious commitment of a hike.

Now, about that "Hard" rating. 14 miles, 2100 feet of gain. That's a full day, possibly more if you're not moving fast. This isn't a casual stroll. You're going to be scrambling, navigating, and putting in the work. Kanab Creek is known for its rugged beauty, which translates to rocky streambeds, some minor boulder fields, and sections where you'll be feeling your way through narrow passages. Expect to use your hands as much as your feet in places. Don't underestimate this one; it’ll push you.

Here's the non-negotiable part: the access road to Kanab Creek is no joke. You absolutely, 100%, without a doubt, need a high-clearance vehicle. Think lifted truck, 4Runner, or something with real ground clearance. My buddy Mark’s Subaru Outback barely cut it on a dry day, and he swore he left half his exhaust pipe back there. Don't be that guy trying to get a sedan down this road. You won't make it, and you'll just piss everyone else off when you get stuck. Seriously, carpool with the friend who has the best rig. Make it a thing.

And yes, it’s a slot canyon, which means we talk about flash floods. Kanab Creek isn't Buckskin Gulch narrow, but it's still a canyon. Keep an eye on the sky, not just where you are, but the entire watershed upstream. If there's any rain up there, even 20 miles away, that water will find its way down here. Always check the NOAA weather for the entire region, not just your immediate spot, before you head out. This isn't just a suggestion; it's how you stay safe in these canyons.

If you're bringing your dog, good news. Riley loves this kind of terrain. He’s an Australian Shepherd, so he’s got the energy for it. Kanab Creek is generally dog-friendly, but remember those scrambles and potential narrow spots. Your dog needs to be agile, comfortable with rough ground, and you need to be prepared to help them over obstacles or through water. And obviously, pack out everything, including their waste. There are no trash cans out here. Keep them leashed, especially around any potential wildlife or other hikers you might encounter.

This is a long day. You want an early start. Like, headlamp-on-the-trail-before-sunrise early. It gives you buffer time, lets you enjoy the cooler morning, and gives you time to deal with any unexpected detours or slowdowns. Pack plenty of water – more than you think you need. There aren't any reliable water sources once you're in the canyon, especially this time of year. Bring snacks, a headlamp even if you plan to be out before dark, and layers. That 49-degree start will feel very different from the mid-70s later in the day. Always double check for any local permits required, though for Kanab Creek it’s typically more about showing up prepared.

An 88/100 for Kanab Creek in early April is a gift. The temps are perfect for covering serious ground, the air is clean, and you won't be battling summer heat or winter ice. You've got the weekend ahead of you. If you’ve been waiting for the right moment to tackle a serious Arizona slot canyon hike, this is it. Get your crew together, find that high-clearance ride, and go earn that post-hike beer. You'll thank yourself later.

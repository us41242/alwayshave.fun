About alwayshave.fun

Real-time trail conditions for hikers, campers, and overlanders across the American West.

What We Do

alwayshave.fun answers one question: should I hit the trail this weekend? We pull live weather, air quality, fire proximity, and river conditions for 40 trails across Nevada, Utah, Arizona, Colorado, and California, then distill it all into a single 1–100 score.

Scores update every 20 minutes via automated data pipelines. No manual guessing, no stale forecasts. Each trail page shows current temperature, wind speed, rain probability, AQI, nearby fire activity, and a 3-day forecast so you can plan ahead with confidence.

Our scoring tiers make it simple: 80–100 means great conditions, 50–79 means proceed with caution, and below 50 means conditions are poor. Even when a trail scores low, we suggest nearby alternatives so you always have a plan.

Our Data Sources

Weather Open-Meteo — temperature, wind, rain probability, and 7-day forecasts
Air Quality AirNow (EPA) with Open-Meteo fallback — real-time AQI readings
Fire Risk NASA FIRMS — satellite-detected fire hotspots and proximity scoring
River Data USGS — stream gauge levels for water-crossing trails

Our Mission

We believe everyone deserves access to clear, up-to-date outdoor conditions without digging through multiple weather apps, fire maps, and air quality sites. alwayshave.fun consolidates all of it into one number and one page per trail.

The name says it all: always have fun. Even when conditions aren't ideal at your first-choice trail, we'll point you toward one where the score is green. There's always somewhere great to go.
